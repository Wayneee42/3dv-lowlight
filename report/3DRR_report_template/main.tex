\begin{abstract}
Low-light 3D reconstruction and novel view synthesis are important for
spatial perception and embodied AI systems, yet they remain highly
challenging due to severe noise amplification, local detail loss, color
distortion, and cross-view photometric inconsistency in captured images.
Under these conditions, the reliable-supervision assumption behind NeRF
and 3DGS no longer holds. More importantly, the reliability of supervision
and priors becomes highly non-uniform across viewpoints, image regions,
and optimization stages. To address this issue, we propose
\textbf{Reliability-Aware Staged Low-Light 3DGS}, a three-stage framework
consisting of geometry bootstrap, reliability-aware geometry refinement,
and geometry-frozen appearance refinement. The first stage aims to build a
stable geometric scaffold, complemented by weak priors. In the second
stage, we continue to reuse COLMAP sparse geometry during training as
quality- and density-aware soft support, so that sparse points with
stronger track consistency and lower reprojection error provide weak
geometric anchoring during no-densify refinement. In the third stage,
after geometry is stabilized, we freeze the main geometric parameters and
perform geometry-frozen appearance refinement in YCbCr space, where luminance
restoration is guided by a calibrated proxy target, while chroma is
corrected against the more reliable supervision signal, supplemented by a
weak residual regularizer. Both branches are modulated by
region-aware confidence weighting. By explicitly respecting the non-uniform
reliability of supervision and priors across both stages and regions, our
framework yields a stable and effective solution for low-light 3DGS
reconstruction and novel view synthesis. Our method was presented in the
NTIRE 3D Restoration and Reconstruction (3DRR) Challenge and substantially
outperformed the provided baselines. The code is available at
\url{https://github.com/Wayneee42/3dv-lowlight}.
\end{abstract}
