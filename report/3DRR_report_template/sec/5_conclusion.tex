\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \textbf{Reliability-Aware Staged Low-Light Gaussian Splatting},
a staged framework for low-light 3D reconstruction and novel view
synthesis. Our method is motivated by the observation that supervision
and priors are not equally reliable under severe low-light conditions.
Instead of treating geometry optimization and appearance refinement in a uniform manner,
we separate the optimization into stages and allocate different
cues to the parts of the pipeline where they are most effective.
Experiments on the RealX3D low-light benchmark show that the
proposed framework achieves the best overall quantitative performance
among the compared methods, while also producing clearer structures,
more plausible brightness, and more natural color restoration
in challenging scenes. We hope this work can serve as even a
slightest bit of reference for broader reliability-aware optimization
in difficult imaging conditions.
